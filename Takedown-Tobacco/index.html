Our Misson

We are team --- from Novi High School in Novi,MI. We started Takedown Tobacco to end a problem plaguing high schools: teenage tobacco use. Scroll down below to see some information about our cause...

CIGARETTES

Smoke inhaled by cigarettes contain over 7,000 harmful chemicals: more than 70 of which are linked to cancer.Cigarette smoking harms nearly every organ in the body. There is NO EVIDENCE that cigarettes advertised as “organic,” “all-natural,” or “additive-free” are any safer or less harmful than other cigarettes.

VAPING

E-cigarettes are found to be the most popular tobacco product among American teens. Most e-cigarettes contain nicotine that can negatively impact adolescent brain development. Side effects include:Increased heart rate and blood pressure, lung disease, chronic bronchitis and insulin resistance leading to type 2 diabetes.

RISKS

Smoking is the leading preventable cause of death in the United States. Both smoked and smokeless cause: various types of cancer, early death from heart disease, health problems in children, increase the risk for a condition called Ventricular Arrhythmias (sudden death from a condition where the heart does not beat properly), and tobacco use harms every organ in the body.

INTERVIEW WITH AN EXPERT


We interviewed an expert in our field to get first person statistics and experience.

Question 1

Q: Our topic is child tobacco usage and our first question is “We know smoking is bad, but can you tell us what exactly happens when we smoke?”


A:So it causes a lot of damage to the lungs and the lining of the lungs. It has the compartments within the lungs and they start breaking and that’s how it causes the damage. So that leads to COPD. That is the first thing that it can lead to. In addition, smoking causes tons of cancers, which doesn’t limit just to lungs, it causes a lot of cancers in the body.



Question 2

Q:How is child tobacco use more dangerous and why?


A:It’s more dangerous, number one because your lungs are still very susceptible and very naïve, and it is starting earlier. Smoking leads to more addictions. So people who start early will end up smoking longer and so all the complications can start happening at a much earlier age.



Question 3

Q:Many people have the misconception that vaping isn’t bad for you. Can you explain how this is untrue?


There are different kinds of vaping. Some of them have a filtered juice and some of them have no filtered juice. So unfiltered juice is much more poisonous, that’s number one, and even if it’s filtered, it has a pretty equivalent amount of nicotine to maybe a little less, depending on the brand and there is no FDA control on that so it can be sometimes more than smoking. And again, the kids who started vaping at an earlier age tend to lead to more addiction problems with other drugs down the road. That’s how it actually can change your brain chemicals too.



Question 4

Q:How severe is the secondhand smoke to children’s health?


A:It’s pretty dangerous. There are kids who grew up before the new laws came about smoking. Kids who grew up with parents smoking in the house, they have had a lot of cancers that smokers have had. So it is as dangerous as smoking. Secondary - secondhand - smoking can be as dangerous as primary smoking in some instances.



Question 5

Q:Do you see any correlation with mental health and the usage of these products among teens?


A:Yes, because what happens is when you are getting addicted, it does change your brain chemicals, and kids who have more anxiety and more depression and more stress, they tend to look for a temporary resort to deal with their symptoms and they do find it does help temporarily but on a long term it does make it worse, but they don’t know and so they get into the vicious cycle and their symptoms can get worse.



Question 6

Q:Is this issue related to family income or lack of parental guidance? teens?


A:Partly, yes and no. Family income, I would say no and parental guidance, to a certain extent yes, to a certain extent no, it depends on the children. A lot of families where they’ve been having open talk about these issues at the family dining table, kids tend to do less, but kids still do it, so yes to answer but not completely one hundred percent full proof.



Question 7

Q:What about cigarettes makes it hard to quit? teens?


A:So cigarettes are again a form of addiction and as any form of addiction worsens the brain cells so it does alter, temporarily kids feel that they are feeling high or they are feeling - they’re forgetting their anxiety issues or - it calms them temporarily, it has a short term impact. So in short, that’s how it does it.

Question 10

A:There is a big stigma about smoking cigarettes because of the fumes and everything. E-cigarettes may not have [the fumes] because it comes in different flavors so people don’t get as dejected, number one, people are not aware of the non-cigarette smoke. They don’t even find out so kids end up doing that more too. So those are the main things and there is myth that when people are trying to quit cigarette smoking they can do better with use of e-cigarettes to curb their craving. So that’s another reason.
